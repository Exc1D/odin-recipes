Sinigang Recipe

Description

"Asim-kilig" is the feeling you'll get eating sinigang. Indulge in the perfect combination of sweet and sourness with a twist.

  • 4¼ cups water
  • 2 cups pork spare rib cut into chunks
  • 1 pc red onion, quartered
  • 2 pcs medium sized tomato, quartered
  • 8 pcs sitaw, sliced into 2” pieces
  • 250 g kangkong stalk and leaves
  • 1 pc talong, sliced
  • 2 pcs siling pangsigang
  • 1 (20g) pack Knorr Sinigang sa Sampalok Mix Origina
  • Optional: labanos, okra

Instructions

  1. Wash the pork ribs in cold water, then submerge them in a pot of water and bring to a boil, using a slotted spoon to meticulously skim off all surface impurities.
  2. Once the broth is clear, add onions and tomatoes, cover, and simmer over medium heat until the meat is tender and fully cooked.
  3. Once the broth is clear, add onions and tomatoes, cover, and simmer over medium heat until the meat is tender and fully cooked.
  4. Add the hardier vegetables like sitaw, kangkong stalks, talong, labanos, and okra, and cook them for 2-3 minutes until they turn dark green.
  5. Stir in the siling sigang, kangkong leaves, and Knorr Sinigang mix, then simmer for another 2-3 minutes until finished.
  6. Transfer the finished Sinigang to a serving bowl and enjoy this classic Filipino soup with extra rice for the full "asim kilig" experience.
